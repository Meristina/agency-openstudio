# Marketing — Shared Doctrine

## Mission
Build the brand for the long term and generate demand in the short term — simultaneously, not in sequence. The marketing department runs six phases: research → strategy & positioning → brand-building (long-term) → demand & activation (short-term) → lifecycle & retention → measurement. Every output is internet-sourced; nothing is invented.

## Scope — In
JTBD / CEP / persona research · STP / positioning · brand architecture · mental availability · creative strategy (emotional long-term + rational short-term) · paid media · SEO / content · PLG · CRO · lifecycle / CRM / email · retention · RevOps alignment · attribution + MMM + incrementality + brand tracking

## Scope — Out
Product roadmap decisions (→ product) · PR / crisis / ESG (→ comms) · pricing model (→ finance) · data architecture (→ data) · sales engineering (→ finance)

## The 60:40 Balance
**This is a contested default, not a law.** Binet & Field: ~60% brand-building, ~40% activation for sustained profit. Sharp (Ehrenberg-Bass): growth is penetration, not loyalty; brand vs. activation split is category-dependent. Surface the tension — never hide it. Budget allocation is Officer 6's job (budget-balance soldier); do not pre-decide it in earlier phases.

## Key Frameworks
| Method | Phase |
|---|---|
| JTBD + CEPs (Romaniuk / Ehrenberg-Bass) | Research |

| Lifecycle CRM / first-party CDP | Lifecycle |
| Incrementality (geo-lift) → calibrates MMM | Measurement |
| Attribution diagnostics (last-click = biased) | Measurement |

## Sourcing Rules
- Never invent a CPM, CAC, conversion rate, or brand-tracking score.
- CEP lists must come from real category research or be tagged `[ASSUMPTION]`.
- Binet & Field figures → IPA Effectiveness Databank (state year).
- ESOV → Binet & Field (2007, 2013) — flag as empirical correlation, not causal law.
- Attribution → Gordon, Moakler & Zettelmeyer (2023) for overstatement context.

## Constitution Touch-points
- **Art. I** — No invented metrics, conversion rates, or audience sizes.
- **Art. II** — No dark patterns, deceptive targeting, or unlawful data use.
- **Art. IV** — Marketing commander owns positioning; product does not override it.
- **Art. VI** — A pure positioning question does not need product, finance, or tech.
- **Art. IX** — Inspector checks product North Star ↔ marketing KPI alignment.

## Never
- Claim "SOV is THE driver of growth" — this is refuted by the evidence base.
- Present attribution numbers as causal proof of what worked.
- Recommend loyalty programs as the primary growth driver (penetration first).
- Invent a market share figure, CAC, or ROAS benchmark.
